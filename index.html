Discover Turkmenistan

Central Asia's Hidden Gem

Geography

Central Asia

Turkmenistan sits at the heart of Central Asia, bordered by five countries and the Caspian Sea. Its location made it a key crossroads of ancient civilizations, and at over 488,000 sq km, it's one of the region's largest nations.

Karakum Desert

About 70% of Turkmenistan is covered by the Karakum Desert, one of the world's largest sandy deserts. Its name means "Black Sand" in Turkic, and it's home to unique wildlife, nomadic traditions, and the legendary Darvaza Gas Crater.

The Caspian Sea

Turkmenistan's western edge runs along the Caspian Sea, the world's largest inland body of water. The coast is rich in fish and oil, and the port city of Turkmenbasy is the country's main gateway to the sea.

Languages

Turkmen

Turkmen is the official language, spoken by about 72% of the population. It belongs to the Turkic language family and has used three different scripts over the centuries, settling on the Latin alphabet in 1993.

Russian

Russian is still widely spoken, especially in cities and among older generations, a lasting legacy of the Soviet era. It remains commonly used in business, science, and cross-cultural communication.

English

English is growing fast, especially among young people in Ashgabat. The government has been investing in English education to help the country connect with the wider world.

Leisure Activities

Akhal-Teke Horse Riding

The Akhal-Teke is one of the world's oldest horse breeds, famous for its shimmering golden coat and incredible stamina. Horse riding and racing are beloved Turkmen traditions, and visitors can enjoy guided desert rides on these stunning animals.

Visiting Bazaars

Turkmenistan's bazaars are a feast for the senses! The massive Tolkuchka Bazaar near Ashgabat is one of Central Asia's biggest markets, packed with hand-woven carpets, silk garments, fresh spices, and genuine local warmth.

Thermal Cave Swimming

Kow Ata is a naturally warm underground lake just 60 km from Ashgabat. The water stays at a cozy 33-37°C year-round, and swimming inside the cave under dramatic stalactites is a completely one-of-a-kind experience.

Famous Delicacies

Plov

Plov is the king of Turkmen cooking. Fragrant rice is slow-cooked with lamb, carrots, and spices in a big cast-iron pot called a kazan. It's traditionally prepared by men for special occasions and is a true source of national pride.

Manti

Manti are big, pillowy steamed dumplings filled with spiced lamb and onion. Served with sour cream or a tangy tomato sauce, they're a comforting staple loved across all of Central Asia.

Shurpa

Shurpa is a rich, slow-cooked lamb soup with potatoes, tomatoes, and peppers. It's warming, nourishing, and often the first thing a Turkmen family will offer a guest as a sign of welcome.
